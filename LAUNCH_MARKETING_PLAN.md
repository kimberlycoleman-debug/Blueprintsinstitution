# THE 6-MONTH LAUNCH MARKETING PLAN
## The B.L.U.E.P.R.I.N.T.S. Discipleship Institute

> *Anchored in the Positioning Document. Built with the founder's heart at the center.*
> *Status: Strategic blueprint for the first 6 months toward Cohort 1 launch.*

---

## OPENING POSTURE

This is not a launch plan in the traditional sense.

Traditional launches treat marketing as broadcast — get attention, drive traffic, convert leads. **That posture would betray what The B.L.U.E.P.R.I.N.T.S. Discipleship Institute actually is.**

This plan operates from a different posture:

🌿 **We are not selling. We are inviting.**
The Institute is free for every believer. There is nothing to buy. People apply because the Spirit drew them, not because we manipulated them.

🌿 **We are not chasing scale. We are stewarding readiness.**
The first cohort matters more than the first 100. The depth of formation in Cohort 1 becomes the testimony that opens every door after.

🌿 **We are not asking permission. We are announcing what God has built.**
Confidence, not insecurity. The category is created. The methodology is proven. The infrastructure is already operational.

🌿 **We are inviting kingdom stewards into something they have never seen before.**
Grant funders and institutional partners are not "donors" or "sponsors." They are stewards. They are funding kingdom infrastructure. They deserve to be honored as such.

## THE SIX-MONTH ARC

**Month 1: FOUNDATION** — Get the public surfaces live. Tell the founding story.

**Month 2: SEED** — Quiet outreach to the right early voices. Build the audience that becomes the chorus.

**Month 3: SIGNAL** — Open applications. Begin grant outreach. First institutional conversations.

**Month 4: GATHER** — Application review. Cohort selection. First grant decisions. Press readiness.

**Month 5: CONSECRATE** — Cohort 1 onboarded. Facilitators trained. Final preparation.

**Month 6: BEGIN** — Cohort 1 launches. The Institute is alive. The testimony begins.

---

## MONTH 1 — FOUNDATION
*"Make the invitation visible."*

### Strategic Goal
Build the public surfaces that allow people to discover, understand, and trust The B.L.U.E.P.R.I.N.T.S. Discipleship Institute. By end of month 1, anyone who hears about Blueprints can find a clear, beautiful, accurate explanation of what it is and how to engage.

### Tactical Workstreams

**1. The Public Website Goes Live**
- `theblueprintsfoundation.org` — public homepage with full transformation promise
- About the Foundation page
- About the Institute page
- The 12-month journey overview (4 quarters explained)
- Statement of faith and theological grounding
- For Individual Believers page (the invitation)
- For Institutional Partners page (the partnership opportunity)
- For Grant Funders page (the case for funding)
- Founder's letter — your voice, your story, why this exists
- Contact / inquiry form
- Application portal (opens later but link reserved)

**2. The Founding Story**
A single written piece — 800-1200 words — telling the origin of The B.L.U.E.P.R.I.N.T.S. Foundation. Not corporate speak. Your actual voice. Why God called this into being. What you walked away from. Who this is for.

This piece becomes:
- The "About" anchor
- The first email to every kingdom steward
- The opener of every grant proposal
- The story journalists will tell when the time comes

**3. The Visual Identity Pack (Already Locked)**
- Logo system — B.L.U.E.P.R.I.N.T.S. with periods, finalized
- Brand canon — warm neutrals, hopeful, human
- Templates for: grant cover, partnership pitch, donor letter, certificate, social cards
- Founder portrait (professional photography session)
- Photography that reflects "every believer" — not stock imagery

**4. The Press Kit (Prepare Now, Use Later)**
- One-page institute summary
- Founder bio
- Trademark and entity structure
- Key statistics about the formation crisis (research-backed)
- Quotes from founder
- High-resolution images
- Contact for press inquiries

### Month 1 Deliverables
- ✅ Public website live
- ✅ Founding story published
- ✅ Visual identity pack finalized
- ✅ Press kit prepared
- ✅ Email infrastructure ready (newsletter, transactional emails)

## MONTH 2 — SEED
*"Build the chorus before the broadcast."*

### Strategic Goal
Identify and engage the first 50-100 voices who will carry this message into their networks. Not influencers — **trusted believers** whose endorsement is real because their relationship with you is real.

### The 50 Voices Strategy

**Identify 50 trusted voices across these categories:**

**Category A: Pastors (15 voices)**
Pastors who have:
- Personal relationship with you or one degree of separation
- A track record of caring about their leaders' formation (not just attendance)
- Cross-denominational openness
- Influence in their region without celebrity-pastor dynamics

**Category B: Christian Marketplace Leaders (10 voices)**
Believers leading businesses, nonprofits, or kingdom enterprises who:
- Have demonstrated they invest in their people's formation
- Carry credibility in marketplace ministry circles
- Are not gatekeepers but bridge-builders

**Category C: Ministry Leaders (10 voices)**
Directors of:
- Christian leadership programs
- Discipleship ministries
- Cross-denominational networks
- Parachurch organizations
who carry credibility without competing with the Institute's mission

**Category D: Formation Practitioners (10 voices)**
Spiritual directors, Christian therapists, inner healing practitioners, Christian coaches who:
- Believe in measurable formation
- Have witnessed the access gap
- Would refer their clients/community to Blueprints

**Category E: Grant Foundation Leadership (5 voices)**
Leaders inside Christian foundations who:
- Have personal relationships with you
- Can give honest pre-feedback on grant positioning
- May become first funders or refer to other funders

### The Outreach Approach

**Not a pitch. A conversation.**

Each of the 50 receives:
1. A personal email or call from you (not a marketing email)
2. A copy of the founding story
3. An invitation to a 30-minute conversation
4. No ask — except: "I'd love your honest reaction and counsel."

**Goal of these conversations:**
- Test the positioning
- Refine the language
- Identify the early supporters
- Discover the first 5-10 institutional partners
- Surface 2-3 potential founding board members or advisors
- Find the first prophets who will say "I see what you're building"

### Month 2 Deliverables
- ✅ 50 voices identified, contacted, and engaged
- ✅ At least 25 conversations completed
- ✅ Positioning refined based on real feedback
- ✅ First 5-10 institutional partnership conversations opened
- ✅ Email list grown to 200-500 engaged early supporters

## MONTH 3 — SIGNAL
*"Open the doors. Send the call. Begin the work."*

### Strategic Goal
Application portal opens. First public announcement of Cohort 1. Grant proposals submitted. First institutional partnership agreements drafted.

### Tactical Workstreams

**1. Public Announcement of Cohort 1**
- Announcement letter to email list (your warm audience from Month 2)
- Founder's video announcement (3-5 minutes, your voice, raw)
- Social posts across owned channels (founder's accounts, Foundation account)
- Press release sent to faith-based media outlets
- Application portal officially opens

**2. The Application Sequence**
When someone submits an application, they enter a thoughtful sequence:
- Confirmation email — "We received your application. Here's what happens next."
- 3 days later — "While we review, here's the story of why this exists" (founding story)
- 7 days later — Application decision (approved / interview / waitlist / declined)
- All decisions handled personally, not algorithmically

**3. Grant Outreach Begins**
Identify and apply to the first 10 grant opportunities. Priority list:

**Tier 1 — Direct relationships:**
- Foundations where you or board members have warm introductions
- Family foundations of major believers in your network
- Denominational foundations aligned with cross-denominational work

**Tier 2 — Mission alignment:**
- Lilly Endowment (religion, formation)
- Murdock Charitable Trust (Christian formation, leadership)
- Maclellan Foundation (Christian discipleship)
- Stewardship Foundation (faith-based formation)
- DeMoss Foundation (Christian formation)
- Local/regional Christian foundations in Illinois and Indiana

**Tier 3 — Innovation funders:**
- Foundations funding faith-tech, evidence-based ministry, measurable outcomes
- Lake Institute on Faith & Giving
- Mortimer & Mimi Levitt Foundation (cultural formation angle)

**For each grant application:**
- Open with the strategic narrative (Section X of Positioning Doc)
- Lead with the founding distinctive (first to measure formation)
- Include the economic model (free for individuals, sustainability through institutions)
- Show the data infrastructure already built (this is unusual for early-stage)
- Request a specific amount tied to specific outcomes

**4. First Institutional Partnership Agreements**
From Month 2 conversations, draft first agreements with:
- 2-3 churches sending leaders through Cohort 1
- 1-2 ministry organizations enrolling staff
- 1 Christian business sponsoring employees

**Institutional pricing:** Set the per-student institutional fee. Recommended structure:
- **Founding Partner rate (Cohorts 1-3):** $2,500-$3,500 per student (discounted to honor early belief)
- **Standard institutional rate (Cohort 4+):** $4,500-$6,500 per student
- **Always:** "Your enrollment of leaders funds free seats for individual believers."

### Month 3 Deliverables
- ✅ Cohort 1 application portal open
- ✅ First 25-50 applications received
- ✅ 10 grant proposals submitted
- ✅ 3-5 institutional partnership agreements drafted
- ✅ Founder's announcement video and letter published

## MONTH 4 — GATHER
*"Choose well. Honor the calling. Steward the moment."*

### Strategic Goal
Review applications carefully. Select Cohort 1 — somewhere between 15-25 students for a manageable founding cohort. Continue grant follow-up. Begin first wave of press outreach.

### Tactical Workstreams

**1. Application Review**
- Every application read personally by you and 1-2 trusted reviewers
- AI-assisted screening summaries (already built into the platform) used to identify themes
- Personal interviews with finalists (30-45 min via video)
- Approval decisions made prayerfully, not just analytically
- Acceptance letters sent personally — this is sacred work

**Cohort 1 size recommendation: 15-20 students.**
Small enough to be intimate. Large enough to be a real cohort. Manageable for first-time facilitators.

**2. Cohort 1 Composition Strategy**
Aim for a diverse founding cohort that becomes a living testimony:
- Mix of marketplace and ministry believers
- Mix of denominational backgrounds
- Mix of ages and life stages
- Mix of "fully resourced" and "couldn't afford otherwise"
- Mix of geographies (testifies to "every believer")

**3. Facilitator Identification and Training**
- Identify 2-3 facilitators for Cohort 1
- Could include you as lead facilitator initially
- Train them on the curriculum, the platform, the formation tracking
- Establish facilitator covenant and accountability

**4. Press Outreach (First Wave)**
Reach out to faith-based media now that Cohort 1 is forming:
- Christianity Today
- Charisma News
- Outreach Magazine
- Relevant Magazine
- Faithfully Magazine
- Religion News Service
- Local Christian publications in your region

**Story angle:** "First formation institute to measure spiritual transformation opens free 12-month program for every believer."

Lead with the founding distinctive. Lead with access. Lead with the new category.

**5. Grant Follow-Up**
- Personal follow-up with every grant officer
- Provide additional materials they request
- Schedule meetings with promising prospects
- Begin building relationships even with grants that decline (long arc)

### Month 4 Deliverables
- ✅ Cohort 1 selected and notified
- ✅ Facilitators identified and beginning training
- ✅ First press features placed or scheduled
- ✅ 2-5 grant proposals advancing to interview stage
- ✅ Founding institutional partners signed

## MONTH 5 — CONSECRATE
*"Prepare what God is releasing."*

### Strategic Goal
Cohort 1 onboarded. Facilitators trained. All systems tested. The Institute is ready to receive students. Prayer cover established. Foundation board reviewing first-year strategy.

### Tactical Workstreams

**1. Cohort 1 Onboarding**
- Personal welcome from founder to each student
- Community covenant signing
- Profile completion in the platform
- Pre-cohort orientation session (live, all 15-20 students together)
- Spirit-led group prayer over the cohort

**2. Facilitator Final Preparation**
- Final facilitator training on all Q1 modules and lessons
- Run-through of the platform's facilitator tools
- Establish weekly facilitator check-in rhythm with you
- Pastoral care protocols established
- Crisis support protocols established

**3. Prayer Cover**
This isn't optional. Build a prayer team:
- 10-20 intercessors committed to praying through Cohort 1
- Weekly prayer updates sent
- Specific prayer needs surfaced
- Founder spiritual covering established (your own personal prayer team)

**4. Foundation Board Review**
- Quarterly board meeting (if board exists)
- Review Cohort 1 readiness
- Review financial position
- Approve next quarter strategy
- Document key decisions

**5. Communications Calendar Established**
For Cohort 1, establish rhythmic communications:
- Weekly student newsletter (lesson preview, encouragement, prayer)
- Monthly institutional partner update
- Quarterly grant funder report
- Bi-monthly donor newsletter
- Founder weekly voice note to cohort (optional but powerful)

### Month 5 Deliverables
- ✅ Cohort 1 fully onboarded and oriented
- ✅ Facilitators trained and confident
- ✅ Prayer cover established
- ✅ Platform tested with real users
- ✅ Communications rhythms ready

## CROSS-CUTTING STRATEGIES

These run through all six months:

### Content Strategy: Founder Voice

**You are the institute's primary voice for the first 12-18 months.** Your story, your conviction, your theology, your warmth — that's what builds trust. Not corporate copy. Not slick marketing.

**Founder publishing rhythm:**
- 1 long-form essay per month (Substack, Medium, or owned blog)
- 1-2 short reflections per week on social (LinkedIn, Instagram)
- 1 podcast guest appearance per month (on shows aligned with your audience)
- 1 founder letter to email list per month
- 1 founder video message per quarter

**Topics that build the category:**
- "Why measuring spiritual formation matters"
- "The blueprint God gave every believer"
- "Why discipleship has to be free"
- "What I learned walking away from a six-figure business model"
- "Identity formation: the missing ingredient in Christian leadership"
- "How the church got formation wrong"
- "What kingdom stewards deserve to see when they fund formation"

### Partnership Development

**The institutional partnership funnel:**
1. Initial conversation (warm intro or cold outreach)
2. Founder-to-founder/pastor-to-pastor meeting
3. Pitch deck shared
4. Discovery of their formation needs
5. Custom partnership proposal
6. Agreement and enrollment

**Target: 5 founding institutional partners by Cohort 1 launch.**

### Grant Strategy

**The grant rhythm:**
- 10 grants per quarter (40 per year minimum)
- Each grant followed up personally
- Quarterly progress reports to all funders (active and prospective)
- Annual impact report distributed widely
- Long-game relationships with foundations (5-7 year arcs)

**Target: $250K-$500K in grant commitments by end of Year 1.**

### Donor Development

**The kingdom steward path:**
- Email list nurtured monthly (story, impact, invitation)
- Major donor identification (15-30 prospects)
- Founder-to-donor personal meetings
- Mid-level donor cultivation (50-100 prospects)
- Annual giving day or campaign (Year 2+)

**Target: $100K-$250K in donor commitments by end of Year 1.**

## METRICS THAT MATTER

These are the metrics worth tracking. Most launches obsess over the wrong numbers. Don't.

### Track These (matter most)

- **Quality of Cohort 1 formation outcomes** (this becomes everything)
- **Institutional partnerships closed** (each one funds free seats)
- **Grants awarded** (each one validates the model)
- **Founder relationships built** (long-term capital)
- **Press coverage in faith-based outlets** (legitimizes the category)
- **Speaking invitations received** (you become the voice of the category)
- **Cohort 1 retention rate** (proof formation is happening)
- **Capstone deliverable quality** (Identity Blueprint Statements, Purpose Statements, Ministry Plans)

### Ignore These (mostly noise)

- Social media follower counts
- Website traffic alone
- Email open rates
- Application volume (volume isn't the goal; right people are)
- General awareness metrics

## THE FOUNDER'S RHYTHM

Your time across these 6 months — protect it.

**60% of your time:**
- Founder relationships (the 50 voices, partners, funders, advisors)
- Founder content (writing, recording, teaching)
- Cohort 1 selection and onboarding

**20% of your time:**
- Platform development and refinement (with your AI developer)
- Curriculum finalization
- Facilitator training

**15% of your time:**
- Strategic decisions and board engagement
- Grant proposal review and approval

**5% of your time:**
- Administrative and operational

**What you should NOT do:**
- Design social media graphics (delegate or use templates)
- Manage applications yourself once they're flowing (delegate intake)
- Write every email yourself (set up workflows)
- Attend every meeting personally (be selective)

**Protect what only you can do.** Everything else can be supported, delegated, or systematized.

## THE HEART CHECK

Before each month, ask yourself these questions:

🌿 **Am I still building from rest, not striving?**
The pace must reflect the message. A formation institute that exhausts its founder during launch betrays its own teaching.

🌿 **Am I honoring the believers who applied?**
Every application is sacred. Every reflection is sacred. Every student is becoming who they were always meant to be.

🌿 **Am I telling the truth?**
Not exaggerating outcomes. Not promising what can't be delivered. Not creating urgency where it doesn't exist.

🌿 **Am I making it easier or harder for every believer to access this?**
Every decision either expands access or contracts it. Choose expansion.

🌿 **Am I trusting God with what I cannot control?**
You cannot control who applies. You cannot control which grants come through. You cannot control which institutions partner. You can control your obedience, your stewardship, and your prayer. The rest is God's.
