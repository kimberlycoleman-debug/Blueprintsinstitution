## ABOUT
*(LinkedIn limit: 2,600 characters)*

I'm building The B.L.U.E.P.R.I.N.T.S. Foundation — a faith-based nonprofit dedicated to identity-centered discipleship and inner healing.

For over a decade, I've worked in healthcare education at Advocate Health Care, teaching sterile processing and witnessing how identity wounds — trauma, shame, rejection — manifest as chronic emotional and spiritual struggles. People sought external solutions for internal crises. Therapy helped. Programs helped. But nothing addressed the root: fractured identity.

That's why I founded The B.L.U.E.P.R.I.N.T.S. Foundation.

What we do: We equip churches, schools, and faith-based organizations with identity-centered discipleship rooted in Scripture and designed for measurable transformation. Our flagship program, The B.L.U.E.P.R.I.N.T.S. Discipleship Institute, is a 12-month formation pathway through identity, inner healing, emotional health, and kingdom calling.

Unlike devotional content or behavior-focused programs, we address the root: helping people discover who God created them to be, dismantle the lies they've believed, and live from their true identity in Christ.

What makes this different: I didn't build this from theory. I built it from testimony. Over seven years I facilitated identity-affirmation workshops and watched God restore what trauma had broken. That work became the curriculum I'm now institutionalizing.

And we measure it differently. Most discipleship assessments are one-time snapshots a person fills out about themselves. We built measurement into the formation journey itself — triangulated across the student's own reflection, a facilitator's trained assessment, and the student's actual written work, tracked from the first lesson to commissioning. Funders can see what their giving produces; partners can see what their people came back with.

What I built: I architected and built the institute's entire platform myself — a production-grade discipleship system on a modern stack (Next.js, TypeScript, Supabase, OpenAI). It runs the full journey end to end: application and screening, the 12-month formation pathway, an AI formation companion that responds pastorally to student reflections, the three capstone builders, commissioning and certificates, and a complete analytics layer — including the Transformation Index, a triangulated measure of formation drawn from student self-assessment, facilitator assessment, and the student's own written work. Most early-stage nonprofits ask funders to help them build the engine. Ours is already built and deployed.

The model: Hybrid social enterprise. Churches and institutions license institutional-grade tools. Individual believers access the program free through grant funding. Institutions pay so individuals don't have to.

Current stage: Launch preparation — building our board, finalizing 501(c)(3) filing, developing partnerships, preparing for Cohort 1. The curriculum is complete and live. The infrastructure is built.

What I'm looking for:
- Grant funders supporting faith-based mental health, discipleship innovation, and identity formation
- Church and ministry leaders seeking transformation tools for their people
- Strategic partners and board members with nonprofit, governance, or faith-sector expertise

If you're funding innovation at the intersection of faith, mental health, and identity restoration — let's connect.
