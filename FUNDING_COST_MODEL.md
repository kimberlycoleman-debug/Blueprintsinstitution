## SECTION 5 — CURRICULUM LICENSE (HOLDING COMPANY): STRUCTURED DEFENSIBLY

**This is the most scrutinized line in your whole model. Read fully.**

### The structure
- **The B.L.U.E.P.R.I.N.T.S. Foundation** (501c3) — runs the institute, receives grants/donations
- **[Your Holding Company]** (for-profit, you own) — owns curriculum IP, licenses it to the Foundation
- The Foundation pays a license fee (benchmarked at $12,000/yr above) to the holding company

### Why it's scrutinized
A nonprofit paying a company the founder owns is a **related-party transaction** ("excess benefit" risk to the IRS). The fear: the founder routing charitable dollars to a personal company. Penalties for getting it wrong range from excise taxes to loss of 501(c)(3) status. Funders look for this on **Form 990 Schedule L**.

### Making it defensible — the checklist
1. **Fair-market value** — the fee must equal what an unrelated nonprofit would pay. Your CPA documents the basis. The $12,000 above is a placeholder; CPA sets the real number.
2. **Independent board approval** — your board (majority independent) approves it; **you recuse yourself** from the vote. Recorded in minutes.
3. **Written license agreement** — real contract, drafted/reviewed by a nonprofit attorney.
4. **Conflict-of-interest policy** — adopted by the Foundation; this deal handled under it.
5. **Form 990 Schedule L disclosure** — transparent reporting. Disclosure is normal; hiding is what causes trouble.
6. **Reasonableness over time** — fee stays tied to fair value as you grow.

### Frame it to funders proactively
> "The curriculum IP is held in a separate entity to protect it and enable future licensing to other ministries. The Foundation licenses it at fair-market value, approved by our independent board with the founder recused, and disclosed on our 990. This protects the asset while keeping our use of charitable funds fully accountable."
